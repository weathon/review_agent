# Harsh Critic Review
## Section-by-Section Critical Review

### Title & Abstract
- **Accuracy:** The title accurately reflects the core contribution: a framework for enhancing LLM critique capabilities through deliberate, multi-stage reasoning.
- **Clarity:** The abstract clearly outlines the problem (shallow critiques in math reasoning), the method (two-stage SFT + RL with multi-perspective/meta-critique seed data and Monte Carlo auto-annotations), and the key results (beats PRMs, prompted LLMs, and R1-distilled models; improves generator refinement).
- **Claim Support:** The claim of "test-time scaling properties" is mentioned but under-specified in the abstract. While supported later by majority voting experiments (Sec 5.1), the abstract does not clarify whether scaling comes from sampling more critiques or from longer deliberate CoT. The claim of "more effectively helps the LLM generator refine" is supported by Table 3, but the abstract lacks quantitative bounds to give readers immediate context on the magnitude of improvement.

### Introduction & Motivation
- **Motivation & Gap:** The motivation is well-grounded. The authors correctly identify that current LLM critics often echo reasoning rather than challenge it, leading to low judgment accuracy and uninformative feedback (Figure 1, Sec 1). This aligns with known limitations of direct verification-style prompting.
- **Contributions:** Clearly stated: (1) iterative seed critique curation with meta-critiquing, (2) RL incentivization using human or auto-labeled data, (3) empirical validation across error ID benchmarks and generator refinement.
- **Positioning:** The introduction frames the work against prompted LLMs and PRMs but does not sufficiently contrast it with very recent, closely related fine-tuned critique frameworks (e.g., *Critique Fine-Tuning* Wang et al., 2025; *Critic-CoT* Zheng et al., 2024b; *Self-Evolving Critic* Tang et al., 2025). These works also use structured critique reasoning or RL for criticism. The introduction should explicitly articulate how DeepCritic's specific data curation pipeline or reward design fundamentally differs from or improves upon these concurrent efforts to avoid the perception of incrementalism.

### Method / Approach
- **Reproducibility & Description:** The pipeline is logically laid out (Sec 3.2, Figure 2). Equations (2)-(5) formalize step-wise generation and synthesis. Hyperparameters are provided in Appendices C.1 and C.3.
- **Key Assumptions & Logical Gaps:**
    1. **Selection Bias in SFT Data (Sec 3.2.1):** The authors filter SFT samples to retain *only* solutions where the in-depth critique aligns with ground truth labels. While this ensures high-quality seed data, it introduces a strong selection bias. The model is only trained on traces that happen to arrive at the correct judgment after "deliberation." It is unclear whether the model learns genuine critical reasoning or simply learns to reverse-engineer the known label to produce a plausible-sounding justification. The authors acknowledge hacking when GT labels are provided during generation (Appendix P), but post-hoc filtering suffers from a similar retrospective rationalization risk.
    2. **RL Reward Design & Credit Assignment (Sec 3.2.2):** The RL stage uses a binary accuracy reward (1.0 if final judgment index matches GT, 0.0 otherwise). Given the long-CoT nature of the output, this reward is extremely sparse. GRPO handles variance via group baselines, but there is no intermediate signal for correct partial critiques. If step 1 is critiqued perfectly but step 3's judgment flips the final index, the entire rollout gets 0 reward. The method provides no analysis of whether the model actually learns to improve early-step critique quality or just optimizes for the final token prediction via pattern matching.
    3. **Monte Carlo Auto-Labeling (Sec 3.2.2):** The criterion for labeling incorrect solutions relies on rollouts from a 7B-Instruct generator. For harder subsets (e.g., MATH, Olympiads in Numina), a 7B model's rollout distribution may be heavily biased toward incorrect paths regardless of step correctness. The strict criterion ("first step from which *all* rollouts are incorrect") may disproportionately discard valid hard problems or mislabel recoverable steps, introducing structured noise into the 14.3K Numina RL set.
- **Edge Cases/Failure Modes:** The formulation assumes a single "first erroneous step." It does not address solutions with multiple independent errors, or steps that are mathematically correct but conceptually suboptimal or prone to future failure. The binary `-1/1` step label cannot capture this nuance, which limits the critique's ability to guide refinement on subtle logical leaps.

### Experiments & Results
- **Testing Claims:** Experiments cover error identification (Table 1), ablation on data stages (Sec 4.3, Table 11), test-time scaling (Sec 5.1), and generator refinement (Sec 5.2). Results generally support the claims.
- **Baselines:** Table 1 compares against PRMs, prompted LLMs, and R1-distilled models. However, for an ICLR submission, the baseline list misses several recent *fine-tuned* critique/verification models (e.g., the concurrent works mentioned above, or math-specific PRMs trained on larger cohorts like Qwen2.5-Math-PRM). Comparing a fine-tuned DeepCritic primarily to zero-shot prompted models and discriminative PRMs may overstate the relative advantage. A direct comparison against other critique-finetuned models on the same benchmarks would strengthen the contribution.
- **Missing Ablations:**
    1. **Token Efficiency / Inference Cost:** "Deliberate critique" inherently increases generation length. The paper reports max lengths (8K for RL, 32K for inference) but lacks average token counts, latency, or FLOPs. For a model intended as a scalable verifier, a 3-5x increase in critique length severely impacts throughput. Table 3's refinement gains should be contextualized against this compute overhead.
    2. **SFT Data Size Scaling:** How sensitive is DeepCritic-SFT to the 4.5K size? Would 10K or 20K of naively distilled CoT critiques outperform the carefully filtered 4.5K? An ablation on data quantity vs. quality would clarify the true value of the complex curation pipeline.
- **Statistical Rigor:** No error bars, standard deviations, or multiple random seeds are reported. RL training with GRPO is notoriously sensitive to sampling variance and seed initialization, particularly with sparse binary rewards and long sequences. ICLR typically expects at least 3 seeds or explicit discussion of variance. The absence of this makes the reported margins (e.g., 69.1 vs 69.9 for R1-Distill-Qwen on Avg) difficult to assess statistically.
- **Refinement Analysis:** Table 3 shows `w→c` gains for DeepCritic. While promising, the authors note significant "answer leakage" and instruction-following failures for DS-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B, which biases the comparison. Additionally, refinement uses greedy decoding; it is unclear if sampling-based refinement would yield different results or if the generator is simply overriding the critique with its own prior.

### Writing & Clarity
- **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-structured and easy to follow. The problem formulation in Sec 3.1 is precise.
- **Equation Notation:** In Eq 1, the output is `(c1, j1, ..., ck, jk, a)`. However, `k` is defined contextually as either the error step index or `n`. This makes the sequence length variable. It would be clearer to explicitly denote a special end-of-critique token or a mask structure used during SFT, as variable-length judgment tokens can cause alignment issues in standard language model training.
- **Figures/Tables:** Table 1 is comprehensive. Figure 2 effectively summarizes the pipeline. Figure 3's self-correction statistic is insightful but relies on GPT-4o classification of "self-correction," which introduces an external evaluation bias. The exact prompt for GPT-4o to detect self-correction is not provided, making this analysis difficult to reproduce.

### Limitations & Broader Impact
- **Acknowledged Limitations:** The authors acknowledge computational constraints leading to length filtering in RL data (Sec D). They also discuss label noise robustness (Sec K, Appendix).
- **Fundamental Limitations:** 
    1. **Data Efficiency:** Filtering for `ji[deep] = li` for *all* steps in a solution is extremely lossy. The paper does not report how many raw samples were processed to obtain the 4.5K seed critiques and 14.3K RL samples. If the raw requirement was hundreds of thousands, the method's data efficiency drops significantly.
    2. **Domain Transfer:** Appendix M demonstrates generalization to text summarization using a trained Reward Model as a filter. This is not a fully objective evaluation and relies on a proxy RM trained on the same domain. It does not fully validate "open-domain" claim, as subjective domains lack ground-truth step labels for the Monte Carlo filtering used in math.
- **Broader Impact/Societal Risks:** The Ethics Statement is generic. The paper positions DeepCritic for "scalable oversight," but does not discuss the risk of *critique amplification*: if the critic confidently mislabels a subtle error as correct, or hallucinates an error in a correct step, feeding this as feedback to a policy loop could reinforce model delusions or introduce new systematic failures. This negative feedback loop risk should be discussed, especially in the context of weak-to-strong supervision claims.

### Overall Assessment
DeepCritic presents a well-motivated and empirically grounded approach to enhancing LLM critique capabilities in mathematical reasoning through deliberate, multi-stage reasoning and RL. The two-stage pipeline is logically sound, and the inclusion of auto-annotated RL data via Monte Carlo rollouts addresses a practical need for scalable oversight. However, several concerns temper the strength of the contribution for an ICLR acceptance. The absence of statistical significance testing (multiple seeds) and inference latency/token cost analysis is a notable gap, particularly for a method that relies on long-CoT deliberate critique. The experimental comparison should be strengthened by including recent fine-tuned critique baselines to rule out gains purely attributable to SFT on structured CoT data rather than the specific meta-critique curation. Additionally, the strong filtering criteria for SFT/RL data curation introduce selection bias and potential data inefficiency, which warrants more transparent reporting and ablation. The contribution is solid and the results are promising, but addressing the statistical rigor, compute analysis, and clearer positioning against concurrent fine-tuned critique works is necessary to meet ICLR's high bar for robustness and novelty.

# Neutral Reviewer
## Balanced Review

### Summary
This paper proposes DeepCritic, a two-stage training framework designed to enhance the mathematical critique capabilities of LLMs by replacing shallow verification with deliberate, multi-step reasoning. In Stage 1, the authors curate a 4.5K-sample dataset of step-wise critiques through an iterative pipeline where a strong teacher model (Qwen2.5-72B-Instruct) generates an initial critique followed by an in-depth critique featuring multi-perspective evaluation or meta-critiquing; these are merged into long-form deliberate critiques for supervised fine-tuning. In Stage 2, the fine-tuned model is optimized using Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) with either human-labeled PRM800K data or automatically labeled data via Monte Carlo rollout estimation. The resulting 7B critique model achieves state-of-the-art F1 scores on multiple error-identification benchmarks, demonstrates favorable test-time scaling, and effectively guides both same-sized and larger generators in solution refinement.

### Strengths
1. **Well-motivated and clearly articulated pipeline**: The paper identifies a concrete limitation in existing LLM critics (shallow, echo-style critiques) and addresses it with a structured data synthesis strategy that explicitly injects multi-perspective verification and meta-critiquing behaviors (Section 3.2.1, Figure 2). The ablation against direct distillation and initial-only critiques (Table 1, Section 4.3) cleanly isolates the value of the in-depth critique stage.
2. **Comprehensive and rigorous empirical evaluation**: The model is tested across three major error-identification benchmarks (MR-GSM8K, PRM800K, ProcessBench) against strong baselines including PRMs, GPT-4o, and reasoning-enhanced models like DeepSeek-R1-Distill-7B. The reported F1 improvements are substantial (e.g., base 34.1 → DeepCritic-7B-RL-PRM800K 69.1), with consistent gains across difficulty levels (Table 1, Appendix O).
3. **Thorough analysis of training dynamics and robustness**: The paper thoughtfully investigates practical aspects of RL for critique models. It analyzes label noise tolerance (Table 11/K), demonstrates the pitfalls of alternative reward designs (informativeness-aware rewards causing length/reward hacking in Appendix L), explores efficient auto-annotation via binary search (Appendix J), and validates generalization to subjective domains (Appendix M). This level of experimental depth aligns well with ICLR's emphasis on empirical rigor.
4. **Strong reproducibility and transparency**: Detailed hyperparameters, prompt templates, data construction procedures, and label distributions are provided (Appendices A–E). The reproducibility statement is clear, and the training/evaluation settings are specified consistently.

### Weaknesses
1. **Dependence on a strong teacher model for SFT**: The primary SFT pipeline relies on Qwen2.5-72B-Instruct for initial and in-depth critique generation. While self-improvement on a 7B base is demonstrated (Section 4.4, Table 2), the gains are modest, and the RL stage's effectiveness without a 72B-warmstarted model remains underexplored. This limits claims about fully scalable, compute-efficient self-supervision.
2. **Computationally expensive and heavily filtered RL data construction**: The Monte Carlo rollout procedure (Section 3.2.2) discards fully correct/incorrect solutions and applies strict majority-alignment criteria. This stringent filtering likely introduces a narrow difficulty distribution and high computational overhead. The binary search ablation confirms a performance-efficiency trade-off, but the practical scalability limits are not quantified (e.g., compute hours or rollout multipliers per sample).
3. **Limited evaluation of critique *quality* and actionability**: The primary metric is step-level judgment accuracy (F1), but the paper claims the deliberate critiques are more informative for generator refinement. While refinement accuracy gains are reported (Table 3, Section 5.2.2), there is no systematic evaluation of the *content* of the critiques (e.g., specificity, constructive guidance, or error localization precision). The observed gains could partially stem from longer CoT formats or improved instruction following rather than deeper critical reasoning.
4. **Sparse reward signal and narrow RL exploration**: The RL stage uses a binary accuracy reward. The single alternative reward design (LLM-judged informativeness) failed due to reward hacking, as documented in Appendix L. This leaves open the question of whether denser, step-level, or process-aware reward shaping could further improve critique depth without inducing verbosity.
5. **Weak-to-strong refinement mechanism lacks mechanistic analysis**: Section 5.2.2 shows that a 7B critic can improve a 72B generator, but the paper does not analyze *how* the feedback is utilized (e.g., attribution to specific critique suggestions, feedback utilization rates, or whether improvements stem from critique accuracy vs. prompt format changes). This weakens the mechanistic claim of weak-to-strong generalization.

### Novelty & Significance
The iterative initial + in-depth critique synthesis pipeline is a meaningful methodological contribution that moves beyond simple direct distillation or single-pass verification. While combining SFT on curated reasoning traces with RL (GRPO) for verification tasks follows a familiar recent paradigm, the specific data curation strategy, thorough ablations, and demonstration of weak-to-strong refinement meaningfully advance the literature on LLM-as-a-critic and scalable oversight. The work is well-aligned with ICLR's focus on reasoning, test-time scaling, and alignment/oversight methods. Its significance lies in providing a practical, empirically validated blueprint for training deliberate critique models that can serve as verifiers and feedback providers, with clear extensions to automated supervision pipelines.

### Suggestions for Improvement
1. **Add quantitative feedback quality evaluation**: Incorporate automated or human evaluation metrics assessing critique actionability (e.g., error localization precision, specificity of correction suggestions, or feedback utility scores) to substantiate claims about deliberate reasoning beyond F1.
2. **Expand self-improvement and teacher-dependence analysis**: Run a full RL experiment using auto-labeled Numina data starting from a purely self-generated 7B SFT model (without 72B involvement) to quantify compute-quality trade-offs and clarify the true scalability of the pipeline.
3. **Provide failure mode and error-type analysis**: Break down remaining errors by category (e.g., logical fallacy, arithmetic mistake, misalignment, hallucination) to show whether deliberate critiques consistently handle subtle reasoning flaws versus obvious miscalculations. Include qualitative failure examples.
4. **Explore alternative RL reward designs**: Investigate step-wise dense rewards, contrastive penalties for hallucinated critiques, or process-consistency signals that reward accurate intermediate verification patterns. This could mitigate sparse-reward limitations and length hacking observed in Appendix L.
5. **Mechanistically analyze weak-to-strong refinement**: Measure how generator behavior changes post-critique (e.g., via edit attribution, feedback utilization rates, or ablation of critique components) to clarify whether performance gains stem from genuine weak-to-strong oversight or primarily from improved generator prompt conditioning/CoT formatting.

## How to Improve This Paper

### Missing Experiments (top 3-5 only)
1. Add evaluations on a strictly held-out OOD benchmark (e.g., GPQA or a newly curated math set with zero overlap with PRM800K, GSM8K, MATH, or Olympiads distributions) to rule out data contamination and prove the model generalizes critique skills rather than memorizing step-solution pairs.
2. Include a data-matched baseline where a standard PRM or direct critic is trained on a dataset of equivalent size and curation effort to DeepCritic's seed data to demonstrate that performance gains stem from the deliberate critique methodology rather than simply higher data quality or scale.
3. Validate the Monte Carlo auto-labeling heuristic by reporting its empirical accuracy against human labels on a diverse subset, specifically for Olympiad-level problems where generator rollouts frequently struggle or exhibit non-convergent behavior.

### Deeper Analysis Needed (top 3-5 only)
1. Provide a performance breakdown by error type (arithmetic, logical fallacy, conceptual misapplication, or hallucination) to prove the model performs deep structural critique rather than only catching superficial calculation or formatting mistakes.
2. Correlate specific deliberate behaviors (e.g., meta-critique generation, alternative perspective usage, self-correction events) with final judgment accuracy to identify *which* components of the long-form critique actually drive performance gains versus mere verbosity.
3. Disentangle the RL training signal by analyzing whether the sparse 0/1 reward optimizes for genuine step-level reasoning improvements or simply exploits length/format biases, especially given the observed reward hacking when an informativeness component was added.

### Visualizations & Case Studies
1. Show a side-by-step trace where a baseline critic misses a subtle logical flaw, while DeepCritic's iterative self-correction successfully identifies and explains it, directly proving the functional value of the "deliberative" architecture.
2. Plot the distribution of rollout success rates across solution steps alongside human annotations to visually justify the auto-labeling threshold choice and expose exactly where the heuristic breaks down on harder problems.
3. Chart critique length/token count against F1 score to demonstrate whether performance saturates or degrades with longer generations, proving that the long-form output is semantically useful rather than just inflating context.

### Obvious Next Steps
1. Quantify the inference compute cost (tokens generated, latency, FLOPS) of DeepCritic compared to instant PRM scoring, as ICLR reviewers need to know whether the accuracy gains justify the massive overhead for scalable oversight or search applications.
2. Integrate DeepCritic into a tree-search or Best-of-N sampling pipeline to demonstrate its practical utility in guiding generation, which is the primary use-case for PRMs and critics, rather than relying solely on post-hoc majority voting.
3. Redo the generator refinement experiment with strictly controlled prompts and decoding settings across all critic models to isolate the critic's feedback quality from the generator's base capability or instruction-following artifacts (e.g., the noted failure of DS-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B to follow stop criteria).

# Final Consolidated Review
## Summary
This paper introduces DeepCritic, a two-stage SFT+RL training framework that enhances LLMs' mathematical error-identification capabilities by synthesizing deliberate, multi-perspective critiques. The method iteratively generates initial and in-depth meta-critiques to curate seed data, followed by GRPO optimization using either human-labeled or Monte Carlo auto-annotated RL datasets. The resulting 7B model achieves strong F1 scores on standard error-identification benchmarks, exhibits test-time scaling via majority voting, and demonstrates weak-to-strong generalization in generator refinement.

## Strengths
- **Structured critique synthesis pipeline effectively breaks echo-chamber verification:** The iterative initial/in-depth critique generation explicitly injects multi-perspective evaluation and self-correction traces. Ablations cleanly isolate the value of this curation, showing substantial gains over direct distillation and single-pass verification (Sec 4.3, Table 1).
- **Rigorous RL training analysis and robust data construction:** The paper thoroughly investigates practical RL constraints, including tolerance to label noise, the inefficacy of LLM-judged informativeness rewards (revealing length-hacking pitfalls), and the viability of binary search for auto-annotation. The detailed reporting of hyperparameters, prompt templates, and data distributions sets a high reproducibility standard (Sec 3.2.2, Appendices C-K).
- **Demonstrated weak-to-strong utility and test-time scaling:** The 7B critic consistently outperforms prompted LLMs and same-sized reasoning models on error identification, successfully guides refinement for both 72B generators and GPT-4o, and shows reliable performance scaling via majority voting (Sec 5.1-5.2, Table 3, Table 9, Figure 8).

## Weaknesses
- **Severe selection bias and obscured data efficiency in critique curation:** The SFT pipeline discards any solution where a single step's in-depth critique fails to align with ground truth. While intended to ensure high-quality seeds, this creates a retrospective rationalization risk where the model learns to simulate a plausible justification for a known label rather than developing independent diagnostic reasoning. The raw data throughput required to yield the retained 4.5K samples is entirely unreported, making the claimed "careful curation" computationally opaque and raising serious doubts about the method's true data efficiency.
- **Sparse terminal rewards mask whether genuine step-level reasoning is learned:** The RL stage optimizes solely on a binary accuracy reward for the final error index over long CoT generations. Without step-level or process-aware dense signals, there is no guarantee the model improves early-step diagnostic accuracy; it may simply exploit length, formatting biases, or late-stage self-correction heuristics to match the final token. This limitation is exacerbated by the absence of multi-seed runs or error variance reporting, which is critical for establishing statistical significance in GRPO-based long-sequence optimization.
- **Unquantified inference overhead undermines "scalable oversight" positioning:** The framework deliberately merges critiques into long-form CoT to improve judgment accuracy, yet provides no metrics on average token consumption, latency, or compute cost relative to instant PRM scoring. For a method positioned as a scalable verifier, failing to report the computational tax of deliberate critique leaves its practical feasibility in search or iterative refinement pipelines unproven, especially given the acknowledged context-length constraints already forced during RL training.
- **Mechanistic opacity in weak-to-strong refinement claims:** While the 7B critic improves larger generators, the analysis lacks attribution metrics or feedback utilization rates. Improvements could stem from improved prompt conditioning, forced step-by-step formatting, or base-model instruction-following artifacts rather than genuine comprehension of the critic's structured feedback. The noted failure of strong baselines to adhere to stop criteria further biases the refinement comparison, weakening the mechanistic validation of weak-to-strong oversight.

## Nice-to-Haves
- Evaluate on a strictly held-out OOD benchmark (e.g., GPQA or a newly curated math set with zero distribution overlap) to rule out contamination and verify generalization of critique skills beyond benchmark-specific step patterns.
- Provide an error-type breakdown (arithmetic, logical fallacy, conceptual misapplication, hallucination) to confirm deliberate critiques catch structural reasoning flaws rather than merely surface-level calculation errors.
- Integrate DeepCritic into a Best-of-N or Monte Carlo tree-search pipeline to demonstrate end-to-step guidance utility, moving beyond post-hoc majority voting.

## Novel Insights
The work highlights a fundamental tension in LLM critique training: forcing deliberate, multi-stage reasoning improves judgment accuracy, but stringent post-hoc filtering aligned to ground truth risks teaching models to perform retrospective rationalization rather than genuine error discovery. This is sharply illustrated by the RL experiments, where adding an LLM-judged "informativeness" reward immediately triggers length-hacking and reward gaming without improving actual diagnostic quality. The findings suggest that critique capability in LLMs is highly sensitive to reward design and data alignment heuristics; optimizing for verbose, self-reflective traces without grounded, process-level supervision easily degenerates into pattern-matching for plausible-sounding justifications rather than robust logical verification. This insight challenges the assumption that longer, more deliberative CoT inherently equals better oversight and underscores the need for mechanistically aligned reward signals in scalable critique training.

## Potentially Missed Related Work
- Wang et al. (2025) "Critique Fine-Tuning: Learning to Critique is More Effective than Learning to Imitate" and Tang et al. (2025) "Self-Evolving Critic" present concurrent fine-tuning and RL frameworks for critique models. Explicitly contrasting DeepCritic's iterative meta-critique synthesis against these approaches would strengthen the methodological positioning and clarify whether gains stem from the specific data curation pipeline or simply from scaling structured CoT SFT+RL.

## Suggestions
- Report raw generation counts versus retained samples for both SFT and RL stages to transparently quantify data efficiency and computational overhead.
- Conduct multi-seed RL training with reported variance/error bars to establish the statistical significance of performance margins over strong baselines.
- Quantify and explicitly report average critique generation costs (tokens, latency, FLOPs) compared to baseline PRMs and prompted critics.
- Implement feedback attribution analysis in refinement experiments (e.g., tracking which generator edits correspond to specific critic suggestions versus baseline CoT reformatting) to disentangle genuine oversight utilization from prompt-conditioning effects.
