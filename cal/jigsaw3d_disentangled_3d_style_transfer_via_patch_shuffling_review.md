# Harsh Critic Review
## Section-by-Section Critical Review

**Title & Abstract**
The title accurately captures the core technique and domain. The abstract claims that the jigsaw operation "decouples style from content" and enables "fast, view-consistent stylization" without per-asset optimization. However, the novelty positioning is slightly overstated: patch shuffling and spatial masking for style-content separation are established in 2D diffusion literature (e.g., StyleAdapter, MAE-inspired style prompts). The abstract should clarify that the contribution lies in adapting and validating this mechanism within a *multi-view 3D conditioning pipeline* and demonstrating its practical utility for UV baking, rather than claiming the jigsaw operation itself as a novel discovery. Additionally, "substantially lower latency" is quantified only later as ~40s; this should be explicitly contrasted with the baseline times in the abstract to ground the efficiency claim.

**1. Introduction & Motivation**
The motivation addresses a real bottleneck: the lack of paired 3D style-texture datasets and the entanglement/inefficiency of SDS or training-free attention fusion methods. The leap from destroying 2D semantic structure via patch shuffling to achieving 3D style disentanglement is intuitively appealing but theoretically underdeveloped. The introduction asserts that local patches preserve "first- and second-order style statistics," but does not address how a diffusion model conditioned on a jigsawed reference learns to project these anisotropic statistics onto a novel 3D geometry without inheriting the reference object's structural biases. Figure 3 quantitatively supports semantic suppression (CLIP drop) and style retention (Gram similarity), but the metric plateau at $N \leq 8$ suggests diminishing returns; the introduction should explicitly justify why $N=8$ is chosen and how this hyperparameter interacts with varying reference image resolutions and texture scales.

**3. Method**
*3.1 Style-Texture Pairs Creation:* The pseudo-pair construction is the paper's core data contribution. A critical concern is potential *content leakage via object identity*. The reference views and target views are rendered from the **same** Objaverse mesh. Even with jigsaw shuffling, the model may simply memorize the base colors/texture distribution of that specific object rather than learning a generalizable style-transfer function. Without cross-object pairing during training, the claim of true style-content disentanglement remains partially confounded by object memorization.
*3.2 Multi-View Style Generation:* Equation 3 defines reference attention as $\text{softmax}(f_{in} f_{ref}^T / \sqrt{d_k}) f_{ref}$. The extraction process for $f_{ref}$ is underspecified. Which self-attention layers of the reference U-Net provide $f_{ref}$? How are multi-scale features aggregated before they serve as K/V? Standard reference adapters project features at multiple resolutions to match the denoiser's spatial hierarchy; omitting this detail hinders reproducibility. Furthermore, the "row-wise self-attention" for multi-view consistency (citing ERA3D) lacks explanation of how camera pose information is integrated. If poses are not explicitly conditioned, the model must infer viewpoint purely from the geometric maps, which may limit robustness on highly irregular or non-manifold meshes.
*3.2.1 3D Style Baking:* The description of UV inpainting as a "weighted average of the nearest neighboring pixels on the object surface" is algorithmically simplistic. In practice, nearest-neighbor or linear blending on complex meshes produces severe blurring across UV seams and occlusion boundaries. Standard texture baking relies on gradient-domain methods, multi-band blending, or Poisson solvers to preserve high-frequency style details. This step is critical for the final output quality, yet its technical formulation is missing, making it difficult to assess whether observed texture smoothness artifacts stem from the diffusion model or the baking heuristic.

**4. Experiments & Results**
*Evaluation Scale & Rigor:* The evaluation set comprises only 20 test meshes and ~70 style references. For an ICLR submission claiming state-of-the-art generalization, this sample size is insufficient to establish statistical significance or robust performance across diverse geometry/topology classes. Table 1 reports marginal improvements on Gram/AdaIN metrics (e.g., AdaIN 113.38 vs 124.54/114.04 on Collected Data), but without variance estimates or significance testing, these differences are difficult to trust given the small $N$.
*Baselines & Metrics:* The CLIP score metric is framed as measuring disentanglement (lower is better). However, the proposed method scores 0.213, nearly identical to MV-Adapter (0.210–0.214), and is outperformed by StyleTex (0.205) which uses explicit text conditioning. This weakens the core claim of superior disentanglement. The qualitative comparison in Figure 4 highlights failure cases in MV-Adapter (e.g., "incorrectly transfers entire texture map layout"), but these appear as cherry-picked examples rather than systematic flaws. A more rigorous evaluation would include a user study or perceptual metrics (e.g., FID, CLIP-IQA) to complement Gram/AdaIN, which are known to poorly correlate with human perception of artistic style.
*Ablation:* Figure 5 provides qualitative ablation of the jigsaw module, but lacks quantitative breakdown. Does removing jigsaw degrade Gram/AdaIN scores by a measurable margin? Appendix A.5 studies patch size and mask ratio, confirming sensitivity, but does not address the training vs. inference patch size mismatch (64×64 vs 128×128). This distribution shift alters the spatial frequency of style tokens fed into the reference attention, potentially causing attention weight miscalibration at inference time.

**Writing & Clarity**
The paper is logically structured and generally clear. However, critical implementation details that dictate reproducibility are missing or vague:
- How are "ground-truth text captions" generated for Objaverse during training? Objaverse captions are notoriously noisy or absent; using them without filtering or a robust captioning pipeline introduces a confounding variable in the style-conditioning pathway.
- The geometric conditioning mechanism ("additive feature modulation" via a T2I-Adapter-like encoder) is referenced but not formally defined. How are the multi-scale features aligned with the U-Net's decoder resolutions?
- Equation 2's masking formulation is visually garbled in the parsed text, though conceptually clear. The main text should explicitly state whether masking occurs before or after shuffling, as this impacts the model's ability to reconstruct directional style cues.

**Limitations & Broader Impact**
The paper appropriately notes SDXL's inability to generate precise text/symbols and acknowledges ethical concerns around IP and cultural appropriation. However, it omits a fundamental limitation inherent to the jigsaw paradigm: **anisotropic and directional styles**. Random patch shuffling destroys stroke directionality, grain orientation, and structural flow. On materials like brushed metal, wood grain, or directional brushstrokes, the model will default to isotropic, patchy textures or fail to align style flows with surface normals. This is a known failure mode of patch-based style isolation and should be explicitly discussed, as it directly limits the method's applicability to a significant class of artistic references.

### Overall Assessment
Jigsaw3D presents a pragmatically motivated pipeline for fast, feed-forward 3D style transfer by leveraging patch shuffling to construct pseudo-paired training data and injecting reference features via cross-attention. The core idea is intuitive and yields promising visual results without per-asset optimization. However, the paper falls short of ICLR's acceptance bar due to significant empirical and technical gaps. The evaluation relies on an unusually small test set (20 objects), making claims of SOTA performance and robust generalization statistically unsubstantiated. The pseudo-pair construction risks object-level memorization rather than true style disentanglement, and key architectural details (multi-scale reference feature aggregation, camera pose conditioning, UV baking heuristics) are underspecified for reproducibility. Additionally, the marginal quantitative improvements over strong baselines and the absence of a quantitative ablation for the core jigsaw mechanism weaken the empirical narrative. If the authors can expand the evaluation scale, clarify the architectural/data pipeline to rule out memorization, address the training/inference patch distribution shift, and rigorously quantify the jigsaw module's impact, the work would be significantly strengthened. As is, the contribution is incremental and lacks the empirical rigor expected for publication.

# Neutral Reviewer
## Balanced Review

### Summary
The paper introduces Jigsaw3D, a feed-forward multi-view diffusion pipeline for fast, view-consistent 3D style transfer. By applying patch shuffling and stochastic masking to reference images, the method disentangles stylistic attributes from semantic content, enabling supervised training on pseudo-paired data synthesized from Objaverse meshes. The framework injects these style cues and geometric conditions (normals/positions) into a multi-branch diffusion U-Net, followed by a robust UV texture baking process that yields high-fidelity, seamless results without per-asset optimization.

### Strengths
1. **Clever Data Construction Strategy:** The jigsaw-based disentanglement elegantly circumvents the scarcity of paired 3D style-texture datasets. By training on synthetically generated pairs, the method bypasses expensive per-scene SDS optimization while enforcing explicit style-content separation. The ablation (Fig. 5) clearly validates its impact on preventing semantic leakage.
2. **High Efficiency & Strong Multi-View Consistency:** Achieves high style fidelity in ~40 seconds, drastically reducing latency compared to iterative optimization baselines (e.g., StyleTex at ~15 min). The integration of row-wise multi-view attention and geometric conditioning consistently produces coherent textures across viewpoints, as demonstrated in multi-object scene and partial stylization experiments (Sec 4.3).
3. **Clear Architectural Integration:** The pipeline logically combines geometry injection, style extraction, and multi-branch attention. The reference-attention module (Sec 3.2) is straightforwardly formulated, and the subsequent style-baking pipeline (visibility-aware reprojection + seam-aware UV inpainting) addresses practical rendering artifacts effectively.
4. **Practical Versatility:** Extends naturally to downstream applications like partial reference stylization, scene-level consistency, and tileable texture generation (App A.6), demonstrating strong real-world utility for 3D content creation workflows.

### Weaknesses
1. **Inadequate Baseline Selection & Comparisons:** The quantitative evaluation lacks comparisons against modern, dedicated 3D texture generation models (e.g., Hunyuan-Texture, Meta3DTextureGen, TriPS). MV-Adapter is primarily a text-conditioned multi-view generator, not a style transfer model, making the comparison misaligned. Additionally, StyleTex represents an older, slow SDS paradigm; newer fast feed-forward stylization methods are absent.
2. **Flawed Disentanglement Metric & Absence of User Study:** The paper claims "a lower CLIP score indicates... more effective disentanglement" between generated views and the reference. This is methodologically weak, as CLIP measures image-text semantic alignment and does not quantify style fidelity or content preservation. For perceptual tasks like style transfer, standard metrics like LPIPS/FID and a formal user study are expected at ICLR to validate qualitative claims.
3. **Missing Dataset Scale & Training Transparency:** The manuscript never specifies the number of Objaverse assets used for training, the total number of synthesized view pairs, or the compute hardware/GPU hours required for the 10-epoch training regimen. These omissions hinder reproducibility and make it difficult to assess the true data/compute efficiency of the approach.
4. **Underdeveloped Theoretical Grounding for Disentanglement:** While Appendix A.2 proves that pixel-level mean/variance are preserved under patch shuffling, diffusion models operate in latent space and rely on high-order feature correlations. The paper does not empirically or theoretically analyze how the jigsaw operation preserves *deep* style features in the pre-trained U-Net, leaving the mechanism somewhat empirical rather than analytically grounded.

### Novelty & Significance
*Novelty:* Moderately novel. Patch shuffling/masking for style disentanglement builds on established 2D style transfer literature (e.g., StyleAdapter, Gatys et al.). However, leveraging this technique to construct a large-scale supervised dataset for multi-view 3D diffusion, combined with a dedicated reference-attention mechanism, represents a meaningful engineering and methodological contribution.
*Significance:* Practically significant. By replacing per-asset score distillation with a supervised, feed-forward diffusion pipeline, the method directly addresses a major bottleneck in 3D content generation (speed, consistency, and cost). The ~40s inference time and robust baking pipeline align well with industry needs for game dev, AR/VR, and digital asset creation, though the incremental architectural innovations may limit broader theoretical impact for ICLR.

### Suggestions for Improvement
1. **Update Baselines & Evaluation Suite:** Compare against recent state-of-the-art 3D texture/stylization models (e.g., Hunyuan-Texture, Meta3DTextureGen) rather than general multi-view generators. Incorporate LPIPS or FID for perceptual quality, and conduct a controlled user study to quantitatively measure style fidelity vs. geometry preservation.
2. **Clarify Dataset & Training Specifications:** Explicitly report the number of training meshes, total generated image pairs, hardware configuration (GPU type/count), and average training wall-clock time. This is critical for ICLR reproducibility standards.
3. **Analyze Latent-Space Disentanglement:** Provide feature-level analysis (e.g., t-SNE/PCA of U-Net latent activations, or style-classifier probing) to rigorously demonstrate that the model learns style statistics from jigsawed inputs rather than relying on memorized priors or hidden semantic cues.
4. **Main-Text Clarifications & Typos:** Move the justification for differing training (64×64) and inference (128×128) patch sizes into the main text, as it critically impacts deployment strategy. Fix the formatting typo in Equation 3 (`softmax(...) / sqrt(d_k)`) to ensure mathematical accuracy for readers.

# Spark Finder Review
## How to Improve This Paper

### Missing Experiments (top 3-5 only)
1. Add a strict out-of-distribution style transfer evaluation using reference images that share zero geometry or viewpoint overlap with any Objaverse training asset. Without it, reported consistency metrics may reflect object memorization rather than genuine, generalizable style extraction.
2. Quantify multi-view consistency using dedicated 3D metrics (e.g., inter-view feature variance, multi-view CLIP similarity, or silhouette/depth IoU) rather than relying solely on 2D perceptual style scores. The core claim of "view-consistent stylization" remains unquantified.
3. Include an architectural ablation replacing the proposed reference-attention module with standard cross-attention, AdaIN, or IP-Adapter-style conditioning. Without proving it outperforms established style injection mechanisms, the architectural novelty and necessity are unconvincing.

### Deeper Analysis Needed (top 3-5 only)
1. Provide explicit feature-space analysis (e.g., t-SNE plots or clustering metrics) demonstrating that jigsawing actually separates semantic and stylistic representations in the diffusion backbone. Relying on VGG Gram/AdaIN is outdated and fails to capture modern diffusion feature dynamics.
2. Quantify geometric preservation by measuring structural deviation (e.g., normal/depth LPIPS or edge alignment IoU) between the original and stylized renders. The paper asserts strong geometric fidelity but provides only qualitative assertions.
3. Analyze the frequency-domain impact of different patch sizes and mask ratios on high-frequency semantic patterns. The failure on text/symbols is dismissed as an "SDXL limitation" without investigating whether the shuffling operation inherently destroys the spatial frequencies needed for coherent fine-grained pattern generation.

### Visualizations & Case Studies
1. Visualize the activation and attention weight maps of the reference-attention module applied to jigsawed vs. raw inputs to prove the network focuses on statistical style patches rather than residual semantic outlines.
2. Show full unwrapped UV texture atlases alongside close-ups of seam intersections to expose how the heuristic reprojection and inpainting steps actually handle occlusion boundaries and blending artifacts.
3. Present a controlled stress-test where a reference image contains highly distinct semantic objects (e.g., a clear photograph of a vehicle) mapped onto a geometrically dissimilar target, visually proving semantic suppression versus leakage.

### Obvious Next Steps
1. Conduct a rigorous user study measuring perceived style similarity, 3D coherence, and geometric preservation across competing methods. Automated metrics like Gram/AdaIN correlate poorly with human artistic judgment, which ICLR reviewers heavily penalize in appearance-focused papers.
2. Report transparent computational accounting, including training GPU-hours, peak memory, and a breakdown of inference latency versus baselines. Claims of "substantially lower latency" are meaningless without side-by-side FLOP and wall-clock comparisons against SDS and feed-forward methods.
3. Benchmark the impact of the heuristic, non-differentiable baking pipeline versus a fully learned 3D-aware texturing approach. The paper presents a heavy 2D-to-3D projection step as a given without quantifying the consistency loss or seams introduced by this architectural shortcut.

# Final Consolidated Review
## Summary
Jigsaw3D introduces a fast, feed-forward pipeline for view-consistent 3D style transfer that eliminates the need for per-asset optimization or curated style-texture pairs. The core innovation is a "jigsaw" operation—spatially shuffling and masking reference image patches to destroy semantic structure while preserving local style statistics—which generates supervised pseudo-pairs from unlabelled 3D assets. A multi-view diffusion model conditioned on these disentangled style cues and geometric maps produces consistent stylized renderings, which are then baked into seamless UV textures.

## Strengths
- **Elegant and Scalable Pseudo-Pair Construction:** The jigsaw transform effectively circumvents the scarcity of paired 3D style datasets by synthesizing supervision from existing assets. By isolating second-order style statistics from global semantics, the method enables efficient supervised training. Qualitative ablations confirm that this mechanism successfully prevents semantic leakage and avoids copying reference object layouts.
- **High Efficiency and Practical Pipeline Design:** The framework achieves high-fidelity stylization in ~40 seconds, offering a drastic speedup over iterative score-distillation baselines. The end-to-end pipeline—integrating geometric conditioning, cross-view attention, and a visibility-aware UV baking step—directly addresses practical rendering artifacts, resulting in seamless, multi-view consistent textures.
- **Strong Style-to-Geometry Projection:** The reference-attention module successfully maps disentangled style attributes to unseen geometries. Results demonstrate accurate region-aware style application (e.g., applying specific color palettes to targeted structural components while keeping others plain) and robust generalization to complex, geometrically sharp shapes in both single-object and multi-scene settings.

## Weaknesses
- **Limited Evaluation Scale and Misaligned Baselines:** The empirical validation relies on a small test set (20 meshes, ~70 styles), which provides insufficient statistical coverage to robustly claim state-of-the-art generalization across diverse topologies. Furthermore, the quantitative comparison omits recent fast feed-forward 3D texturing/stylization pipelines, comparing instead against slow optimization-based methods or general multi-view generators that lack dedicated style-transfer training. This misalignment makes the reported performance margins difficult to interpret relative to the current landscape.
- **Risk of Object Memorization in Data Construction:** Training pairs are generated by rendering multiple views of the *same* mesh. While the jigsaw operation suppresses immediate semantic cues, this setup risks the model learning object-specific priors or trivial geometry-to-color mappings rather than a generalizable, view-agnostic style projection function. The absence of cross-object pairing or a strict out-of-distribution evaluation leaves the claim of true content-style disentanglement partially empirically unverified.
- **Questionable Disentanglement Metric and Reproducibility Gaps:** The paper relies on a CLIP-score metric to claim style-content disentanglement (where lower is better), yet the proposed method scores competitively with baselines and is outperformed by a method using explicit text prompts, weakening the core empirical claim. Additionally, critical implementation details are underspecified: the exact U-Net layers aggregated for reference features ($f_{ref}$), the scale of the training dataset, and the justification for the distribution shift between training (64x64) and inference (128x128) patch sizes are missing from the main text.

## Nice-to-Haves
- Include a quantitative breakdown of the jigsaw module's impact on style fidelity metrics to complement the qualitative ablation.
- Incorporate perceptual alignment metrics (e.g., LPIPS) or a controlled user study to validate style fidelity, as Gram/AdaIN statistics correlate imperfectly with human perception of artistic quality.
- Provide a feature-space analysis to rigorously demonstrate that the diffusion backbone learns true style statistics from jigsawed inputs rather than relying on hidden geometric priors.
- Benchmark the reference-attention module against standard style injection techniques (e.g., AdaIN, IP-Adapter style conditioning) to isolate its architectural necessity.

## Novel Insights
The paper's core insight—using a spatial jigsaw transform to decouple style from semantic content for 3D style transfer—is pragmatically powerful. By demonstrating that patch-level shuffling destroys global object structure while retaining the statistical priors necessary for diffusion conditioning, the authors unlock a scalable, supervised training paradigm that bypasses the need for curated style pairs or slow score-distillation optimization. This approach fundamentally shifts 3D stylization from a test-time optimization problem to a feed-forward generation task, offering a highly efficient blueprint for integrating 2D diffusion priors into practical 3D asset creation workflows.

## Suggestions
- Expand the evaluation suite to include recent feed-forward 3D stylization or texturing baselines, and supplement the CLIP-disentanglement metric with perceptual alignment scores to ground architectural and performance claims.
- Report complete training specifications (dataset scale, compute budget, exact feature extraction pipeline for $f_{ref}$) in the main text to ensure reproducibility, and explicitly analyze how the training-inference patch size mismatch affects attention weight calibration.
- Add a dedicated discussion and analysis of the jigsaw operation's structural limitations on directional/anisotropic styles (e.g., brushstrokes, wood grain) and fine-grained patterns, explicitly framing these failure modes as a consequence of spatial frequency disruption rather than solely attributing them to the diffusion backbone.
