# Harsh Critic Review
## Section-by-Section Critical Review

### Title & Abstract
The title accurately reflects the method and focus. The abstract clearly states the problem (limited generalizability of vector-based implicit ICL), the proposed solution (In-Context Routing/ICR), and the main empirical claims (train-once-reuse, consistent OOD improvement, zero-shot efficiency). However, the claim that ICR *"internalizes generalizable ICL patterns"* leans heavily on empirical transfer rather than mechanistic verification. While plausible, "internalization" implies a deeper alignment with how the model structurally represents ICL, which the current analyses only partially support. I recommend tempering this phrasing or strengthening the mechanistic evidence in the paper.

### 1. Introduction
The motivation is well-grounded: explicit ICL is costly and brittle, while current implicit methods are post-hoc residual perturbations tied to task-specific distributions. The transition to attention-logit steering is intuitively justified by pointing to the centrality of Q/K interactions in MHA. Figure 1 effectively illustrates the instability of multi-task explicit prompting, setting up the need for a cleaner, structural intervention. The research question and three contributions are clearly delineated. No major concerns here.

### 2 & 3. Method / Approach (Attention Routing & ICR)
**Technical Soundness & Reproducibility:**
- **PCA Centering Omission (Sec. 2.2):** The extraction of Principal ICL Directions (PIDs) via PCA on stacked $Q$ and $K$ bases is central to the method. PCA theoretically requires mean-centering the data before computing covariance/eigenvectors. The paper does not specify whether the ICL bases were centered. If uncentered, the first principal component may merely capture the mean activation direction rather than the variance structure critical for routing. Please clarify this implementation detail.
- **Logit Magnitude & Softmax Saturation (Eq. 3 & 10):** $\Delta A_l$ is directly added to pre-softmax attention logits. The original logits scale with $\|Q\|\|K\|/\sqrt{d_k}$, which is typically $\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{d_k})$. The paper uses $\tanh(\alpha)$ and cosine annealing to bound routing vectors, but does not analyze the absolute magnitude of $\Delta A_l$ relative to the base logits. If $\Delta A_l$ dominates, it can saturate the softmax, effectively hardening attention and potentially disrupting early-layer syntactic processing. A brief analysis or empirical distribution of $\|\Delta A_l\| / \|A_{l,h}\|$ would strengthen confidence in training stability.
- **Theoretical Assumption Validity (Sec. 2.3 / App. A):** The Spiked Covariance + pooled PCA argument relies on the assumption that domain-specific components $B_{q,d}$ are "sufficiently diverse and lack consistent alignment," allowing them to average toward isotropy. While mathematically clean, NLP tasks often share spurious lexical or syntactic correlations (e.g., frequent question words, common punctuation). The robustness of the PID extraction to such structured domain overlap is untested. A brief discussion or ablation on correlated vs. orthogonal task domains would ground the assumption.
- **Router Bottleneck (Sec. 3.2):** The router maps a 384-dimensional frozen MiniLM embedding of a *query-only* zero-shot input to $L \times (r + H)$ routing parameters. For Llama2-7B, this implies mapping a single semantic vector to ~600 gating/weight values. While the MLP has sufficient capacity, the semantic alignment between a frozen sentence encoder and layer/head-specific attention dynamics is non-trivial. How sensitive is routing performance to the quality of the encoder? Appendix G.5 partially addresses this (mpnet vs. miniLM), but the inherent capacity mismatch remains a plausible failure mode for highly specialized or semantically ambiguous queries.

### 4. Experiments & Results
**Experimental Rigor & Baselines:**
- **OOD Baseline Protocol Ambiguity (Sec. 4.1 & Table 1):** This is the most critical experimental concern. The paper claims ICR operates *"without retrieval or retraining"* on OOD tasks, yet compares against I2CL, LIVE, and M²IV, which are described as methods *"that involve calibration or training with data."* How are these baselines configured for OOD evaluation? If they are trained/calibrated using OOD labels or demonstrations, the comparison violates standard zero-shot OOD evaluation and artificially favors ICR. If they are forced to use only cross-domain ID vectors, this should be explicitly stated, as it explains their high "Collapse" counts. The current phrasing leaves the fairness of the OOD comparison ambiguous.
- **Statistical Significance Missing:** Table 1 reports averages over 3 seeds but omits standard deviations or confidence intervals. Given that OOD gains on some datasets are modest (e.g., +1.5% to +3%), and many tasks use only 500 test samples, these differences could fall within variance bounds. ICLR standards typically require error bars or significance testing (e.g., paired t-test or bootstrap across seeds/datasets) to substantiate claims of *"consistent outperformance."*
- **ID Performance vs. Few-shot:** The claim that ICR *"closely matches and can even surpass few-shot prompting on ID tasks"* is partially supported but slightly overstated. On Llama2-7B (Table 1), ICR underperforms few-shot on TREC, CSQA, and PIQA. The gap narrows or reverses on Qwen2.5, suggesting model-dependent behavior. I recommend explicitly acknowledging this variance and discussing whether ICR's zero-shot efficiency trade-off is model-architecture sensitive.

### 5. Analyses & Limitations
- **Interpretability Claims (Sec. 5.1 & Table 16):** The "ICLness tokens" analysis is intriguing but leans speculative. The scoring metric heavily weights cross-dataset consistency, and the resulting top tokens include many non-content words, foreign language stems (`forme`, `zusammen`, `trouv`), and dataset-specific entities (`court`, `dep`). While the authors argue against trivial functional words, the connection to "reasoning-oriented structures" remains tenuous without deeper attention map visualization or causal intervention (e.g., masking these tokens degrades performance?). I recommend reframing this as an exploratory observation rather than strong mechanistic evidence.
- **Hierarchical Analysis (Sec. 5.3):** The layer/head/PID importance analysis is the strongest qualitative section. Figure 4 convincingly shows shared hub layers, consensus heads, and task-aligned PID correlations. This directly supports the claim that ICR adaptively composes routing paths rather than applying uniform steering. Well done.
- **Limitations & Broader Impact:** The paper does not explicitly discuss limitations. Key unaddressed limitations include: (i) dependency on high-quality initial PID extraction (requires diverse multi-task ICL prompts), (ii) potential failure on tasks requiring explicit demonstration content rather than routing structure (acknowledged briefly in App. K but not in main text), and (iii) router reliance on frozen encoder quality. ICLR expects a dedicated limitations subsection; adding one would strengthen the manuscript.

---

### Overall Assessment
This paper proposes a novel and conceptually elegant direction for implicit ICL by shifting from post-hoc residual vector injection to structured, low-rank modulation of attention logits. The method (ICR) is theoretically motivated through a spiked covariance framework, practically instantiated with a lightweight query-conditioned router, and empirically validated across multiple models and a diverse set of OOD tasks. The analyses of layer/head routing dynamics are particularly insightful. However, for ICLR acceptance, the authors must address several critical gaps: clarify the exact OOD evaluation protocol for baselines to ensure fair comparison; provide statistical significance measures for the claimed improvements; specify PCA centering and analyze the magnitude of logit biases relative to base attention scores to ensure softmax stability; and add a limitations section addressing encoder bottlenecks and task dependencies. The contribution is strong and aligns well with ICLR's interest in mechanistic understanding and efficient adaptation, but the empirical rigor and methodological transparency require tightening before publication.

# Neutral Reviewer
## Balanced Review

### Summary
The paper introduces In-Context Routing (ICR), an implicit in-context learning framework that replaces residual-stream vector injection with structural modulation of attention logits. By extracting cross-task Principal ICL Directions (PIDs) via PCA and employing a lightweight query-conditioned router, ICR creates a train-once-and-reuse system that steers zero-shot inference. Extensive evaluations across 12 benchmarks and multiple LLMs demonstrate that ICR consistently outperforms prior vector-based implicit ICL baselines and exhibits robust out-of-domain generalization without task-specific retrieval or retraining.

### Strengths
1. **Conceptual Shift to Attention-Space Modulation:** The paper cleanly identifies the limitation of post-hoc residual steering and proposes operating directly on query-key interaction geometry (Sec 2.2). The low-rank bias formulation (Eq. 3, 10) is mathematically principled and distinct from additive shift vectors, offering a more structural mechanism for implicit ICL.
2. **Strong Empirical Performance & OOD Robustness:** Results across 12 tasks show ICR surpasses baselines on average and exhibits zero "collapse" cases on OOD datasets (Table 1). The framework generalizes seamlessly from 5 in-domain tasks to 7 near/far OOD tasks (including CB, COPA, AI2SciE) where multi-task few-shot prompting fails, validating the train-once-and-reuse claim (Sec 4.2, App E).
3. **Solid Theoretical & Interpretability Grounding:** The use of a mixed spiked covariance model and Davis-Kahan perturbation bounds (Sec 2.3, App A.3-A.4) provides rigorous justification for why pooled PCA recovers shared ICL structure while averaging out domain-specific noise. Hierarchical analyses (layer/head/PID importance, Fig 4) and "ICLness" token probing (Sec 5.1, App H) transparently connect routing mechanics to model behavior.
4. **Comprehensive Ablations & Design Validation:** Systematic experiments isolate the contribution of each component (Table 3, 4), confirm that OOD transfer specifically requires PCA-aligned directions rather than generic low-rank projections, and justify critical choices like late-layer intervention and last-token pooling (App G). This rigor strengthens confidence in the method's robustness.

### Weaknesses
1. **Limited Theoretical Analysis of the Router:** While PID extraction is backed by random matrix theory, the query-conditioned router's training lacks formal guarantees. There is no analysis of convergence, loss landscape properties, or bounds on how router weights generalize to unseen query distributions beyond empirical OOD results (Sec 3.2-3.3).
2. **Narrow Evaluation Paradigm:** All benchmarks rely on fixed-label, multiple-choice or classification tasks evaluated via next-token scoring over constrained candidate sets (App D.3.1). Generalization to open-ended generation, instruction-following, or tasks without discrete label spaces remains untested, limiting claims of broad "domain diversity" applicability.
3. **Incomplete Efficiency & Architectural Positioning:** The efficiency analysis (App F, Table 11) focuses on cached parameter size and compares inference time mainly to few-shot prompting. It lacks a granular breakdown of per-sample latency, memory overhead, and FLOP comparisons against zero-shot baselines and PEFT methods like LoRA, making deployment trade-offs less clear.
4. **Ambiguity in "Internalization" Claim:** The paper argues ICR "internalizes" ICL, yet the method still relies on externally computed subspaces and a trained router module. From an architectural standpoint, this closely parallels parameter-efficient adaptation (e.g., prompt tuning or low-rank adapters), and the functional distinction requires deeper discussion to avoid overstating novelty relative to established lightweight adaptation techniques.

### Novelty & Significance
- **Novelty:** High. Moving from residual-stream additive vectors to attention-logit routing via cross-domain PCA extraction represents a meaningful paradigm shift in implicit ICL. The formulation is well-grounded and extends theoretical framing beyond heuristic post-hoc steering.
- **Clarity:** High. The paper is logically structured, notation is consistent, and figures/tables are highly informative. Theoretical derivations, method description, and experimental results are presented cohesively.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate-High. Training protocols, hyperparameters, dataset splits, templates, and hardware setup are detailed (App D). Code is promised but not yet released; providing it pre-review or in a supplementary zip would strengthen this score. Some OOD evaluation specifics (e.g., exact prompt phrasing for AI2SciE) are only partially tabulated but remain reconstructible.
- **Significance:** High. ICR directly addresses core ICL bottlenecks: inference latency, prompt instability, and poor cross-task transfer. The single-training-cycle, retrieval-free design offers a practical, scalable pathway for deploying LLMs in dynamic environments, aligning strongly with ICLR's emphasis on robust generalization and efficient representation learning.

### Suggestions for Improvement
1. **Theorize the Router's Generalization:** Add a brief analysis of the router's training dynamics, such as gradient flow properties through the frozen backbone, Lipschitz continuity of the routing function, or empirical calibration curves showing how router confidence correlates with OOD accuracy.
2. **Expand Efficiency Benchmarking:** Provide explicit wall-clock latency, VRAM peak, and FLOP counts per sample for ICR versus zero-shot, explicit 5-shot, and LoRA across varying sequence lengths (e.g., 256, 1024, 2048). A breakdown of router vs. attention-modification overhead would clarify real-world deployment costs.
3. **Test on Open-Ended Tasks:** Include at least one generative or free-form benchmark (e.g., short summarization, mathematical reasoning, or instruction following) to demonstrate whether attention-logit routing generalizes beyond constrained label spaces.
4. **Clarify Architectural Relationship to PEFT:** Add a dedicated discussion or table comparing ICR to prompt-tuning and low-rank adapters in terms of parameter placement, gradient routing, and theoretical expressivity. Explicitly delineate why attention-logit modulation offers unique advantages over weight-space or input-embedding adaptations.

# Spark Finder Review
## How to Improve This Paper

### Missing Experiments (top 3-5 only)
1. **Leave-one-domain-out training for the router.** The router is trained on all 5 ID datasets; train on a strict subset (e.g., 3 domains) and evaluate on the held-out ID and far-OOD tasks to prove the claims of true cross-task generalization rather than co-adaptation to a fixed training mix.
2. **Stronger PEFT and modern mechanistic baselines.** The LoRA comparison only ranks the classification head; add competitive full-attention LoRA, prompt-tuning variants (e.g., P-Tuning v2/LP), and recent activation-steering methods to validate whether ICR truly "outperforms parameter-efficient alternatives" or just benefits from weaker baselines.
3. **Controlled robustness of PID extraction.** The method requires few-shot prompts across multiple domains to build ICL bases; test PID extraction using only 1-2 domains, unbalanced class sampling, or noisy demonstrations to verify if the "train-once-reuse" framework collapses without idealized, curated extraction data.
4. **Open-ended generation evaluation.** All datasets use constrained multiple-choice/binary formats scored on next-token logits; evaluate on free-form generation (e.g., instruction following, summarization, math) to prove attention routing enhances semantic reasoning rather than simply exploiting fixed candidate token spaces.

### Deeper Analysis Needed (top 3-5 only)
1. **Empirical validation of the spiked covariance assumption.** The theory claims domain-specific variations average out to isolate shared ICL subspaces; quantify the actual eigengap and measure how much PCA-extracted PIDs align with known ICL mechanisms (e.g., induction heads, copy-matching) versus generic linguistic statistics.
2. **Mechanistic verification of attention redistribution.** The paper asserts ICR "reroutes intra-query attention to structural roles" but shows only aggregate accuracy; compute attention map similarity metrics (e.g., KL-divergence, attention flow overlap) between ICR, zero-shot, and explicit few-shot to prove it actually replicates ICL dynamics.
3. **Quantifying the frozen encoder bottleneck.** The router depends on a separate MiniLM encoder that may poorly encode far-OOD domains; replace MiniLM with the LLM's own zero-shot token representations or stronger encoders in an ablation to isolate whether OOD limits stem from the routing design or the input embedding quality.
4. **Sensitivity of extracted PIDs to backbone weights.** PIDs are extracted from one model but applied to others; measure subspace alignment (e.g., canonical angles) across different checkpoints to determine if "generalizable ICL patterns" are truly architecture-agnostic or highly model-specific artifacts.

### Visualizations & Case Studies
1. **Side-by-side attention heatmaps (Zero-Shot vs. ICR vs. 5-Shot).** Visualize full-layer or representative-head attention matrices for challenging far-OOD prompts to expose whether ICR functionally emulates demonstration-driven routing or merely shifts logits on superficial structural tokens.
2. **Failure case breakdown on far-OOD tasks.** Since OOD average gains over zero-shot are modest (often 1–4%), explicitly examine inputs where ICR fails or underperforms few-shot, revealing whether the method breaks on factual grounding, multi-hop reasoning, or long-range dependencies that require demo content rather than routing priors.
3. **Cross-domain PID correlation matrix beyond aggregate accuracy.** Extend the PID importance correlation visualization to include all OOD extraction combinations, explicitly showing whether PIDs capture task-agnostic reasoning axes or cluster tightly with the original 5 training datasets.

### Obvious Next Steps
1. **Dynamic layer intervention driven by router outputs.** Instead of hardcoding intervention in the last 1/3 of layers, use the router's predicted layer importance scores to adaptively select or skip operations, directly translating the hierarchical analysis into an efficiency improvement and proving actionable routing signals.
2. **Compatibility testing with Mixture-of-Experts or non-standard architectures.** The method broadcasts a low-rank bias to all heads assuming dense MHA; validate or adapt the routing formulation for MoE or grouped-query attention models to address practical deployment limits for modern LLMs.
3. **Systematic ablation of extraction prompt engineering.** PIDs are built from carefully balanced, manually templated few-shot prompts; vary demonstration count, ordering, and template phrasing during extraction to quantify how tightly PID quality depends on brittle prompt design choices that contradict the "generalizable" premise.

# Final Consolidated Review
## Summary
This paper introduces In-Context Routing (ICR), an implicit in-context learning framework that replaces post-hoc residual vector injection with structural modulation of attention logits. By extracting cross-task Principal ICL Directions (PIDs) via PCA and employing a lightweight, query-conditioned router, ICR creates a train-once-and-reuse system that steers zero-shot inference. Extensive evaluations across 12 benchmarks and multiple LLMs demonstrate consistent improvements over prior vector-based implicit ICL methods, with robust out-of-domain generalization and zero performance collapses below the zero-shot baseline.

## Strengths
- **Conceptually grounded shift to attention-space modulation:** The paper cleanly identifies the structural limitations of additive residual steering and proposes directly modulating query-key interaction geometry via low-rank logit biases (Eq. 3 & 10). This formulation is mathematically principled, explicitly tied to MHA mechanics, and represents a clear paradigm shift in implicit ICL design.
- **Robust empirical transfer without task-specific adaptation:** Results across 12 diverse datasets show ICR surpasses vector-based baselines on average and achieves zero "collapse" cases on far-OOD tasks (Table 1). The train-once-and-reuse design successfully decouples adaptation from demonstration retrieval or per-task fine-tuning, validating its practical utility.
- **Rigorous isolation of core design choices through ablations:** Systematic experiments confirm that OOD transfer critically depends on PCA-aligned directions rather than generic low-rank projections, that late-layer intervention preserves early syntactic processing, and that balanced, multi-domain extraction pools are essential for capturing shared invariances (Tables 3-5, App G). This depth of analysis strongly supports the methodological validity of ICR.

## Weaknesses
- **Under-delineated novelty relative to parameter-efficient tuning:** The paper positions ICR as a mechanism that "internalizes" ICL patterns, yet functionally it shares strong similarities with prompt-tuning and low-rank adapters: both employ a small set of frozen, learnable parameters guided by an input-conditioned router. The architectural and theoretical distinction between attention-logit biasing and established weight/embedding-space PEFT methods is not rigorously articulated, which risks overstating the mechanistic novelty relative to the broader adaptation literature.
- **Narrow evaluation paradigm constrains generalization claims:** All 12 benchmarks are constrained multiple-choice or binary classification tasks evaluated via next-token log-probability over fixed candidate sets. While this is standard for ICL probing, it limits assessment of whether attention routing truly enhances semantic reasoning or simply optimizes over narrow, structured label spaces. Generalization to open-ended generation, free-form instruction following, or tasks without discrete candidate tokens remains untested, tempering claims of broad "domain diversity."
- **Missing dedicated limitations section and slight claim calibration:** The manuscript lacks an explicit limitations subsection (a standard requirement for ICLR), relegating important constraints (e.g., router dependency on frozen encoder quality, failure modes when tasks require explicit demonstration content rather than routing structure) to appendices. Additionally, claims that ICR "closely matches and can even surpass few-shot prompting" on in-domain tasks are slightly overstated; Table 1 shows ICR trails few-shot on several ID benchmarks, and the trade-off should be framed more precisely.

## Nice-to-Haves
- Provide attention-map similarity metrics (e.g., KL-divergence or attention flow overlap) between zero-shot, ICR-augmented, and explicit few-shot prompts to mechanically verify the "attention routing" hypothesis beyond aggregate accuracy scores.
- Include a leave-one-domain-out evaluation for router training to rigorously decouple task-agnostic generalization from co-adaptation to the fixed 5-domain training mix.
- Clarify the exact configuration of OOD baselines in the main text (e.g., explicitly stating they use ID-trained vectors without any OOD-specific calibration) to eliminate ambiguity in the transfer protocol.
- Briefly specify whether PCA extraction utilizes mean-centering or SVD on raw covariance for full reproducibility.
- Add error bars or bootstrap significance estimates across the 3 seeds and 500-sample evaluations to quantify the statistical reliability of modest OOD gains (e.g., +1.5% to +3%).

## Novel Insights
The paper successfully reframes implicit ICL not as a post-hoc residual correction, but as a structural reparameterization of the query-key matching geometry. By demonstrating that cross-task Principal ICL Directions can be extracted, compressed into a low-rank subspace, and dynamically routed via input-conditioned logit biases, the work reveals that a substantial portion of few-shot ICL's benefit stems from learning stable, task-agnostic attention routing priors rather than merely ingesting demonstration content. This mechanistic insight provides a concrete, architecture-agnostic pathway for distilling demonstration-induced attention dynamics into a retrieval-free, zero-shot adaptation mechanism.

## Suggestions
- Add a dedicated "Limitations" subsection to the main text explicitly addressing the closed-set evaluation paradigm, router sensitivity to frozen encoder quality, and scenarios where explicit demonstration content is irreplaceable.
- Calibrate in-domain performance claims to accurately reflect where ICR trails vs. surpasses few-shot prompting, and provide a concise architectural comparison table contrasting ICR with prompt-tuning and LoRA to firmly ground its novelty within the PEFT/implicit adaptation literature.
- Prioritize attention-flow visualization or similarity analysis as a post-revision addition to directly substantiate the claim that ICR replicates ICL routing dynamics at the attention map level.
