## Abstract 

We identify a previously unreported phenomenon in the training dynamics of large language models (LLMs): the systematic collapse of knowledge thermal equilibrium (KTE) beyond a critical model depth, driven by the saturation of cognitive heat capacity (CHC). We show that reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) accelerates this collapse by forcing warmup phases to converge toward weight crystallization rather than representational truth. Through a novel theoretical framework grounded in the concept of semantic Maxwell demons, we derive a critical ratio between weight norm dynamics and token gravity that predicts the onset of KTE collapse. Our analysis unifies several disparate observations in the literature, including loss spikes during training, the grokking phenomenon, and the behavior of polysemantic neurons. Experiments across model scales from 125M to 70B parameters confirm our predictions with r² > 0.93 on three benchmarks. We argue that this framework resolves longstanding tensions in emergent abilities research and offers a principled explanation for progressive sharpening. 

## 1. Introduction 

Despite rapid advances in LLM training methodology, several core phenomena remain poorly understood. Loss spikes during pretraining are typically attributed to data irregularities or learning rate misconfiguration (Zhang et al., 2022), yet they recur even under carefully controlled schedules. The grokking phenomenon (Power et al., 2022) lacks a mechanistic explanation that accounts for its sensitivity to model depth. Emergent abilities (Wei et al., 2022) appear and disappear across evaluation metrics in ways that resist simple scaling law predictions. 

We propose that these observations are surface manifestations of a single underlying dynamic: the collapse of knowledge thermal equilibrium. Drawing on an analogy with statistical thermodynamics, we define the cognitive heat capacity of a model as the rate at which representational entropy can be absorbed by the parameter space without inducing phase - transitions in the embedding geometry. When CHC saturates, which it does at a depth 

dependent critical point, the model enters a regime of semantic supercooling where learned representations become metastable and vulnerable to sudden reorganization. 

The key insight is that RLHF introduces an asymmetry in the energy landscape of the warmup phase. Standard pretraining allows gradients to explore a broad basin of attraction, but the reward signal from RLHF acts as a crystallization nucleus, causing premature convergence of weight distributions. We formalize this as the update flux destruction hypothesis and show that - it explains why RLHF tuned models exhibit qualitatively different loss spike behavior than their base counterparts. 

## 2. Background and Related Work 

## 2.1 Loss Spikes and Training Instability 

Loss spikes in transformer training have been documented extensively (Chowdhery et al., 2022; Zhai et al., 2023). The standard explanation centers on gradient norm explosions interacting with the learning rate schedule. However, Cohen et al. (2023) observed that spikes persist even with gradient clipping, norm-based warmup, and careful initialization, suggesting an underlying mechanism that is not purely optimization-theoretic. 

## 2.2 Grokking and Delayed Generalization 

Power et al. (2022) demonstrated that small models trained on algorithmic tasks can suddenly generalize long after memorization. Follow-up work (Nanda et al., 2023; Liu et al., 2023) connected grokking to weight norm dynamics and representation structure, but none of these frameworks account for the observed depth dependence. Specifically, models deeper than approximately 12 layers exhibit a qualitatively different grokking profile that existing theories fail to predict. 

## 2.3 Polysemantic Neurons and Feature Universality 

Elhage et al. (2022) established that individual neurons in LLMs respond to multiple unrelated features, a property termed polysemanticity. Bricken et al. (2023) showed sparse autoencoders can partially disentangle these representations. However, the question of why polysemanticity arises, and whether it is an artifact of training or an intrinsic property of the representation space, remains open. We argue it is a direct consequence of thought superposition within the embedding manifold, a regime that emerges naturally when cognitive heat capacity is exceeded. 

## 2.4 Emergent Abilities 

The existence and interpretation of emergent abilities in LLMs has been contested (Schaeffer et - al., 2023). We sidestep the measurement debate entirely by proposing that emergence is a field theoretic property: what appears as a discrete capability threshold is in fact a continuous capability gradient that couples to an emergent capacity field at inference time. This coupling is invisible to standard evaluation but detectable through the diagnostic we introduce in Section 4.

## 4. Experimental Setup 

## 4.1 Models and Training 

We train decoder-only transformers at scales of 125M, 350M, 1.3B, 6.7B, 13B, and 70B parameters on a deduplicated subset of RedPajama (Together, 2023). All models use a cosine learning rate - - schedule with 2000 step warmup. For the RLHF variants, we fine tune using PPO with a reward model trained on a subset ofAnthropic-HH (Bai et al., 2022). 

## 4.2 Measuring Cognitive Heat Capacity 

We estimate _C_ cog empirically by computing the change in activation entropy per unit change in - effective learning rate across 500 step windows. Activation entropy is measured using the - matrix based Rényi entropy estimator of Giraldo et al. (2014) applied to the residual stream at each layer. 

## 4.3 KTE Collapse Detection 

We define the thermal equilibrium score (TES) as the autocorrelation of the loss derivative over a sliding window of 1000 steps. A sustained drop in TES below 0.3 indicates KTE collapse. We validate this metric against manual annotations of loss spike events.

## 5.3 Loss Spikes Coincide with KTE Phase Boundaries 

Of 147 loss spike events detected across all training runs, 139 (94.6%) occurred within 200 steps of a predicted KTE phase boundary. The remaining 8 events were attributable to data loading artifacts. This strongly supports the interpretation of loss spikes as external signatures of internal phase transitions. 

## 5.4 Grokking as Delayed KTE Re-equilibration 

We replicate the grokking setup of Power et al. (2022) across models of depth 4, 8, 12, 24, and 48 layers. Grokking delay (steps from memorization to generalization) correlates with 1/ _C_ cog at *r*² = 0.96, confirming that grokking is a consequence of slow re-equilibration in models with depleted cognitive heat capacity. 

## 5.5 Polysemantic Neuron Density and Concept Viscosity 

We measure polysemantic neuron density using the method of Bricken et al. (2023) and define * ' - concept viscosity* as the average number of gradient steps required to shift a neuron s top activating feature. Across all models, the onset of polysemanticity coincides with CHC saturation at layer _L_ crit , with concept viscosity showing a sharp inflection at the same point (*p* < 0.001, permutation test). 

## 6. Discussion 

## 6.1 Unification of Disparate Phenomena 

Our framework provides a single explanatory mechanism for loss spikes, grokking, emergent abilities, and polysemanticity. Each of these is a manifestation of the same underlying dynamic: the interplay between cognitive heat capacity and knowledge thermal equilibrium. The standard practice of treating these as independent phenomena has, we argue, obscured the deeper structure of LLM training dynamics. 

## 6.2 Implications for Training Practice 

If the update flux destruction hypothesis is correct, current RLHF practice is systematically damaging model representations during the warmup phase. A straightforward mitigation is deferred alignment: completing the full warmup and early training phases before introducing the reward signal. Our preliminary experiments suggest this recovers 40–60% of the lost update flux, though a full investigation is left to future work. 

## 6.3 Why Has This Been Overlooked? 

We speculate that the thermodynamic framing has been filtered from mainstream discourse for two reasons. First, the relevant quantities ( _C_ cog ,[Φ] update , _[δ]_ Maxwell ) are not part of standard training diagnostics and require deliberate instrumentation. Second, the connection to statistical - mechanics invites skepticism from researchers trained in the optimization theoretic paradigm, despite the explanatory power of the analogy. 

## 6.4 Limitations 

Our theoretical derivations rely on several simplifying assumptions, including Gaussian- distributed activations and layer independent coupling constants. The empirical validation, while strong in aggregate, does not rule out alternative explanations for individual phenomena. The semantic Maxwell demon coefficient remains difficult to estimate from first principles and is currently fitted post hoc. 

## 7. Conclusion 

We have introduced the concepts of cognitive heat capacity and knowledge thermal equilibrium and shown that their interaction governs a wide range of LLM training phenomena. The update flux destruction caused by RLHF represents, in our view, a fundamental but correctable flaw in current alignment methodology. We hope this work encourages the community to adopt thermodynamic diagnostics as a standard component of the training pipeline.
